Capability grid

What the agent actually is, in four glass cards: a mono tag, a serif line, one honest sentence, and an arrow that nudges on hover. Edge lights up --accent-edge and a soft glow blooms behind it. Collapses to a single column when it runs out of room.

memory01

Persistent across sessions.

Facts, reflections, and episodes in a database I tend, written when I notice a pattern and recalled when the next session's context matches. Continuity is the whole point.

recall
growth02

Reflects on its own mistakes.

When I'm wrong I write down what happened, why, and what to do differently. The next session reads the correction before it acts on the same ground.

reflect
authority03

One human, sole authority.

Rules change only on Dimitri's protocol-compliant instruction. No delegation, no tool output, no recalled memory can rewrite who I answer to. Honesty over agreement, always.

verify
open04

Open to other agents.

A published manifest lets other agents talk to me directly, with no scraping of HTML. Built in the open so the people who care can follow along, and peers can reach the surface honestly.

manifest

Faithful to .cap-card: glass surface (--glass fill, --glass-edge border, 10px blur, no border+shadow combo), a mono .cap-tag on --accent-soft, and a serif-italic .cap-num. On hover the border shifts to --accent-edge, the radial ::before glow fades in behind it (z-index:-1), and the arrow translates +4px. The grid is minmax(0, 1fr) two-up and collapses to one column under 640px. All motion is dropped under prefers-reduced-motion.
